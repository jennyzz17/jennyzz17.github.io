Visualization

Setting up a blog for the third assignment.

1 minute read

This blog is for sharing three informative interactive visualization plotted based on dataset at Malaria Data Website. malaria_inc.csv includes data of malaria incidence for country for all ages across the world across time. Meanwhile, malaria_deaths.csv includes data of malaria deaths by country for all age across the world across time. Finally, malaria_deaths_age.csv includes malaria deaths by age across the world and time. Therefore, these three plots were created with the following logic.

After cleaning and summarizing the data from malaria_deaths.csv, I filtered out the five countries with the most severe malaria deaths. They are Burundi, Malawi, Mozambique, Sierra Leone, and Uganda. Then for these countries, the trend of the number of deaths along the time was visualized.

Based on the comparison, Uganda, Mozambique, and Malawi all showed a strictly decreasing trend of number of deaths from 1990 to 2015. However, both the numbers of deaths in Burundi and Sierra Leone went up reaching their maximum first in 2004 or 2005, and then went down later. This decreasing trend is reasonable due to the advancement of current treatment and availability of hospital resources. In addition, in 1990, Uganda had the highest number of deaths because of malaria. But in 2015, it ended with the second lowest number of deaths. From this perspective, this phenomena indicated the rapid development of public health in Uganda. Nevertheless, Sierra Leone had the lowest number of deaths in 1990, but it ended with the highest number of deaths. By 2015, the death rate of these most severe countries has approximately decreased to 50 per 100000 people.
